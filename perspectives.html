Perspectives about Rhino Poaching



Who are the groups involved in this issue?

The poachers
Doctors who use it for medicine
The people who buy them on the black market




Are there conflicts/arguments and disagreements about the issue?

There are big disagreements because some of the natives feel that the white people just strolled in, fenced in the rhinos they were able to hunt, and made laws against it. This makes some of the indigenous people disapprove of the laws and try to break them.



Explain people's differing viewpoints on the issue

Some people don't want to stop hunting/poaching animals like rhinos as they give medicine from their horns, and others don't want to kill them and just harvest the horns.



Do some groups of people get impacted by the issue more than others? Explain why.

Yes, some groups of people will get impacted by this issue because if they are hunters and they can't hunt anymore or if they don't hunt but people are still out there hunting/poaching.




Have there been some solutions in the past that have failed or succeeded by different groups or individuals?

Pembient, which is a US-biotechnology start-up that aimed to act as "the De Beers of synthetic wildlife products." They tried to manufacture and sell 3D printed rhino horn with the aim of a cheaper alternative and to reduce the incentive to hunt live animals, but this whole thing failed to go mainstream, with some saying it may hurt the animals more.
